## Who This Guide Is For

You've heard affiliate marketing can generate passive income. You want to try it but don't know where to start. You have under $100 to invest. This guide is for you.

## Step 1: Pick a Niche

Don't pick what you love. Pick what people spend money on and search for online.

Good niches have three things:
- **Buyer intent keywords** — people searching "best X for Y" are ready to buy
- **Products with affiliate programs** — not everything pays commissions
- **Content you can realistically create** — you need to write or record about this weekly

Examples that work in 2026: AI tools, email marketing software, website builders, online course platforms.

Examples that don't work: broad categories like "technology" or "health" — too competitive for a new site.

## Step 2: Set Up Your Website

You need three things:
1. A domain name (~$12/year)
2. Hosting (free with Vercel or Netlify if you use a static site generator)
3. A blog platform (Astro, WordPress, or Ghost)

Total cost: $12 for the domain. Everything else can be free.

## Step 3: Write Your First 5 Articles

Before applying to any affiliate program, you need content. Most programs reject empty sites.

Write these five article types:
1. **"Best [category] tools for [audience]"** — your main listicle
2. **"[Tool A] Review"** — in-depth review of your top pick
3. **"[Tool A] vs [Tool B]"** — comparison of two popular options
4. **"How to [solve problem] with [tool]"** — tutorial showing the tool in action
5. **"What is [category]? A beginner's guide"** — informational post for SEO

## Step 4: Apply to Affiliate Programs

Once you have 5 articles live, apply to affiliate programs for the tools you reviewed. Most SaaS tools have programs accessible through their website footer or a search for "[tool name] affiliate program."

Expect some rejections. Apply to 10, get accepted by 5-7. That's normal.

## Step 5: Add Affiliate Links to Your Content

Replace your placeholder links with real affiliate links. Place them:
- In your "verdict" or "final thoughts" section
- In comparison tables
- At natural decision points where the reader is ready to act

Don't overdo it. 2-3 links per article is enough. More feels spammy.

## Step 6: Drive Traffic

Your articles need readers. Two main channels:
- **SEO** — optimize for the keywords you targeted. Results take 3-6 months.
- **Short-form video** — create 30-second demos on TikTok and YouTube Shorts. Send viewers to your articles.

## What to Expect

- Month 1-3: Building content. Minimal traffic. $0 revenue.
- Month 4-6: Google starts ranking your articles. First commissions trickle in.
- Month 6-12: Compounding. Each new article adds to the total. $200-800/month is realistic.

The people who fail are the ones who quit in month 2 because nothing happened yet. The system works, but it works slowly.
