## What I liked

**The funnel builder actually works.** Drag-and-drop, templates that don't look like 2015, and the pages load fast. I built a complete opt-in funnel in under 40 minutes on my first try.

**Email deliverability is solid.** I ran a test campaign to 500 contacts. Open rate: 34%. For context, industry average is 21%. The emails landed in primary inbox on Gmail, not promotions.

**Everything is connected.** When someone opts into your funnel, they're automatically added to your email list, tagged, and can be enrolled in an automation. No Zapier. No manual exports. It just works.

## What I didn't like

**The automation builder feels limited.** If you're coming from ActiveCampaign or ConvertKit, the automation triggers feel basic. No conditional splits based on email engagement beyond opens and clicks.

**No native A/B testing for emails.** You can A/B test funnel pages but not email subject lines. For a marketing tool in 2026, this is a notable gap.

**The affiliate dashboard is functional but ugly.** It works, but the UI feels like it was designed in 2020 and never updated.

## Who should use Systeme.io

You're a freelancer or coach selling digital products or services. You're currently paying for ConvertKit ($29), a landing page tool ($29), and maybe a course platform ($39). That's $97/month for three tools. Systeme.io replaces all three for $27.

## Who should skip it

You run an e-commerce store — Shopify is better. You need advanced CRM — HubSpot is better. You have a team of 5+ people managing marketing — you'll outgrow it.

Systeme.io is the most underrated tool in the SaaS space right now. It's not the best at any single thing, but it's good enough at everything that matters, and the price-to-value ratio is unmatched.
