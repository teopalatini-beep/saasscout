## Our Top 3 Quick Picks

1. **Moosend** — Best for budget automation. Full automation engine at $9/month.
2. **Beehiiv** — Best for newsletters. Built for growth with free plan up to 2,500 subs.
3. **ConvertKit** — Best for creators. Visual automations with a loyal creator community.

## What We Tested

We signed up for 12 email marketing platforms, built a welcome sequence on each, sent test campaigns, and measured deliverability across Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo. We also timed how long it took to complete common tasks: building a signup form, creating a 3-email automation, and segmenting a list. The 7 tools below survived real use.

## 1. Moosend

**Best for:** Small businesses that need automation without the price tag.

Moosend gives you conditional automations, segmentation, landing pages, and unlimited emails starting at $9/month. The automation builder rivals ActiveCampaign's — conditional splits, delays, multiple triggers — but costs a fraction. Deliverability tested at 94% inbox placement across our campaigns.

**Pricing:** Free (1,000 subs), Pro from $9/month (500 subs).

**The catch:** The interface looks dated and the integration library is small. If you use niche tools beyond Shopify and WordPress, you'll need Zapier.

## 2. Beehiiv

**Best for:** Newsletter creators focused on growth.

Beehiiv isn't a traditional email marketing tool — it's a newsletter platform. Built by ex-Morning Brew employees, it focuses on subscriber growth: referral programs, recommendation networks, and SEO-optimized web versions of every email. If your business model is "build an audience through email," Beehiiv is purpose-built for that.

**Pricing:** Free (2,500 subs), Scale $39/month (100K subs), unlimited on higher tiers.

**The catch:** Limited automation compared to traditional email tools. No visual automation builder. If you need complex conditional sequences, Moosend or ConvertKit are better. Beehiiv is for newsletters, not marketing funnels.

## 3. ConvertKit (now Kit)

**Best for:** Creators who sell digital products.

ConvertKit built its reputation serving bloggers, podcasters, and course creators. The visual automation builder is clean and powerful. Tagging and segmentation are intuitive. And the commerce features — selling digital products and subscriptions directly — eliminate the need for Gumroad or Payhip.

**Pricing:** Free (1,000 subs, limited automations), Creator $25/month (1,000 subs, full features).

**The catch:** $25/month for 1,000 subscribers is expensive. Moosend does the same automation for $16/month at the same list size. ConvertKit's edge is the creator ecosystem and commerce features — if you don't sell digital products, you're overpaying.

## 4. MailerLite

**Best for:** Beautiful emails on a budget.

MailerLite combines strong design tools with affordable pricing. The drag-and-drop editor produces modern-looking emails without touching code. Templates are clean and contemporary — better than Moosend's library. Add landing pages, pop-ups, and a website builder at no extra cost.

**Pricing:** Free (1,000 subs, 12,000 emails/month), Growing Business $10/month (500 subs, unlimited emails).

**The catch:** The free plan limits you to 12,000 emails/month. Hit that and you're forced to upgrade. Automation exists but isn't as deep as Moosend or ActiveCampaign. Good for simple sequences, limited for complex logic.

## 5. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for:** Businesses that need email + SMS + chat in one tool.

Brevo combines email marketing with SMS, WhatsApp, and live chat. If you communicate with customers across multiple channels, Brevo consolidates everything into one dashboard. The email builder is solid and the CRM is surprisingly capable for a marketing tool.

**Pricing:** Free (300 emails/day), Starter $25/month (20K emails), Business $65/month (unlimited contacts).

**The catch:** Pricing is by email volume, not contacts. Confusing if you're used to per-subscriber pricing. At high volume, costs escalate quickly. And the free plan's 300 emails/day cap makes it hard to test with a real list.

## 6. ActiveCampaign

**Best for:** Businesses that need advanced automation and CRM.

The deepest automation engine in email marketing. Site tracking, lead scoring, conditional content, predictive sending — ActiveCampaign does things no other tool on this list can. If your email strategy involves complex conditional logic based on user behavior, this is the tool.

**Pricing:** Lite $29/month (1,000 contacts), Plus $49/month (CRM + lead scoring).

**The catch:** Expensive, complex, and overkill for most small businesses. If you're sending a weekly newsletter and a welcome sequence, you're paying for 80% of features you won't use. Start here only if you've outgrown simpler tools.

## 7. Mailchimp

**Best for:** Beginners who want the largest integration ecosystem.

Mailchimp integrates with everything — 300+ native integrations. Every SaaS tool, every e-commerce platform, every CRM connects to Mailchimp without Zapier. The brand recognition also means great documentation and community support. For someone starting from zero, the learning resources are unmatched.

**Pricing:** Free (500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month), Essentials $13/month (500 contacts, 5,000 emails).

**The catch:** The free plan is barely usable now — 500 contacts and 1,000 emails is gone in a week for any real business. Pricing increased 30-40% in the last year. Automation is locked behind higher tiers. The value that made Mailchimp famous has eroded significantly.

## Bottom Line

**On a tight budget:** Moosend ($9/month) for automation, MailerLite ($10/month) for design.

**Building a newsletter:** Beehiiv (free) is purpose-built for audience growth.

**Selling digital products:** ConvertKit ($25/month) has commerce built in.

**Need multi-channel (email + SMS + chat):** Brevo ($25/month) consolidates everything.

**Complex automation needs:** ActiveCampaign ($29/month) if you've outgrown basic tools.

Don't start with the most powerful tool. Start with what matches your actual workflow today and upgrade when you hit limits.
