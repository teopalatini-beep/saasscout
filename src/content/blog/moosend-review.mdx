## TL;DR

**Rating: 7.5/10** — The best email marketing tool if your budget is tight but you still want real automation. Not as polished as Mailchimp or ConvertKit, but at $9/month for 500 contacts with unlimited emails, nothing else comes close on value.

**Who it's for:** Solopreneurs, small businesses, and bloggers who want automation workflows without paying $30+/month for ConvertKit or ActiveCampaign.

**Who should skip it:** Agencies managing multiple clients, creators who need beautiful templates out of the box, or anyone already happy with their current tool.

## What is Moosend

An email marketing platform with automation, landing pages, and basic e-commerce tracking. Think of it as "80% of ConvertKit at 30% of the price."

No free forever plan — just a 30-day trial. But the Pro plan starts at $9/month, which is cheaper than most tools' free tiers once you actually need features.

**The plan most people should pick:** Pro at $16/month for 1,000 contacts. It includes everything — automations, landing pages, transactional emails. No feature gates between tiers.

## What I Liked

**Automation builder punches way above its price.** You get conditional splits, delays, tagging, and multi-step sequences — the same stuff ActiveCampaign charges $49/month for. I built a 5-step welcome sequence with conditional branching in under 20 minutes. The visual builder is clean and intuitive.

**No feature gating between plans.** Every paid plan gets the same features. The only difference is contact count. This is rare — most competitors lock automations, A/B testing, or landing pages behind higher tiers. With Moosend, a $9/month user gets the same tools as a $32/month user.

**Deliverability is surprisingly good.** I sent a test campaign to 300 contacts across Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo. 94% inbox placement. For a budget tool, that's excellent — I've seen more expensive platforms do worse.

**Reporting is detailed without being overwhelming.** Open rates, click maps, device breakdown, geolocation — all in a clean dashboard. You don't need to export to a spreadsheet to understand what's working.

## What I Didn't Like

**The template library is mediocre.** About 80 templates, and most look dated. You'll either need to start from scratch or import your own HTML. ConvertKit and Mailchimp have much better starting points.

**No free plan anymore.** They used to have one. Now it's just a 30-day trial. If you're testing multiple tools before committing, the clock is ticking. Beehiiv and MailerLite still offer usable free tiers.

**The landing page builder is basic.** It exists, but the templates are limited and the editor feels clunky compared to dedicated tools. Don't pick Moosend for landing pages — pick it for email.

## Who Should Use Moosend

You need automation workflows but can't justify $29+/month yet. You're a blogger or solopreneur with under 5,000 contacts. You care more about what emails do (automations, sequences, triggers) than how they look (templates, design).

At the $9-16/month price point, no other tool gives you this level of automation capability.

## Who Should Skip It

You need gorgeous email templates without custom coding. You want a free plan to test indefinitely. You're an agency managing 10+ client accounts — the interface isn't built for that workflow.

If design matters more than automation, look at MailerLite. If you want a free forever plan, look at Beehiiv for newsletters or MailerLite for traditional email marketing.

## Final Verdict

Moosend is the best-kept secret in email marketing. The automation engine rivals tools costing 3-4x more, deliverability is solid, and there's zero feature gating. The trade-off is dated templates and no free plan. If you're willing to design your own emails or use simple text-based formats, Moosend saves you serious money while giving you enterprise-level automation.
